Wiring

Battery → XIAOspring contacts
Cell snaps between the spring plate (below, on the printed seat) and the flat plate (above, in the stem base). Plate lugs → slide switch (on the + line) → XIAO BAT+ / BAT− pads; the BAT− wire runs up its own Ø1.6 channel beside the stem bore. No solder on the cell, ever. Charging via the Type‑C under the clip — leave the switch on to charge.
OLED → XIAO4 wires
I²C: 3V3 · GND · SDA · SCL to the XIAO's default I²C pins.
Camera → XIAOplug‑in
Flex ribbon into the Sense camera socket. No wiring — it's the OV5640 module made for this board.
Antenna → XIAOplug‑in
Snap the U.FL plug onto the board connector; the flat antenna sits in the −Y bay, up high and away from the cell.

Assembly notes

Enclosure

Two drop‑in halves

Splits lengthwise; parts drop in, the halves clamp them. No screws — the clip's socket wraps a split central boss on the cap, squeezing the two half-cores together (that's the clamp), and the rubber grip band clamps the barrel; alignment pins register the seam. Push the clip on last; pull it off to open.

Camera

Octagon window + rail cradle

Looks out a square‑based octagon window on the cap; the square can is wider than the window, so it can't push out. ±X rails locate it (lens ↔ window), and the flex runs down the open −Z/−Y side to the board. Swing the clip either way to uncover the lens. Get a short flex (~25–30 mm) — the module's stock 78 mm flex is far too long to fold into the cap (only one ~29 mm fold fits beside the can).

Screen

Bezel‑lip mount

The OLED drops into its +Y pocket and seats behind a bezel lip — the window is smaller than the module, so it can't fall out the front. A strip of double‑sided tape holds it against the lip; the camera can is secured the same way.

Battery

Off‑centre cell

The 10440 sits 0.7 mm toward −Y so it clears the OLED on +Y; three cradle rings centre it and notch the wire path.

Antenna

Its own bay

The board cage is opened on −Y so the U.FL plug, flat antenna, and coax have room — far from the metal cell.

Wire path

Up the barrel

OLED / switch / battery leads run up the annular gap beside the cell, through the stem bore, into the board bay.

Safety

Protection built in

The protected cell handles over‑discharge — no separate board (it wouldn't fit). Charge only over the XIAO's USB‑C.
